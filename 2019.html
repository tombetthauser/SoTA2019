SotA Survey 2019

An Open-Information Census for Visual Artists

State of the Arts Survey:

  • What cities are artists moving to?
  • What kinds of jobs are we working?
  • How are we sharing our artwork?
  • How can we better support ourselves?
  • In what ways are we struggling?
  • In what ways are we thriving?

State of the Arts is an anonymous census-style survey intended to help artists gather and have free open access to critical information regarding trends in the arts communities of which they are a part. The goal of the survey is to assist artists in practical decision making and aid them in building a more inclusive, sustainable and vibrant art world by giving them free access to this information.

The survey will remain open until the end of 2019 at which time the anonymous data gathered will be made 100% public and freely accessible for all artists to draw their own conclusions and insights from. Accessibility will take the form of raw cross-referenceable data as well as more standard graphs and charts.

  • Not made for profit or publicity. Built freely for artists by artists.

Who Should Take it & Why?

The questions on this survey were designed to serve all forms of visual artists, from art students to visual arts professors and established artists with international exhibition records.

If you have ever found yourself struggling in the decision making process of changing cities, changing jobs, applying for graduate school or making any practical decision to help better support and enrich your art practice, then this survey is for you.

If you Can, Pass it Along!

This survey will only be helpful to us as individuals and communities if as many of us as possible participate. Since this is a non-profit community-based project it needs your help. If you want to support the project please consider passing it along!

  • Share it on social media

  • Tell your students or teachers

Plans for Open Source:

When the 2019 survey is complete, all the anonymous data collected and the platform built for displaying that data will be made 100% open source for artists to freely access and use. Additionally as an open source project, artists will be welcome to participate in the development of the codebase and the survey questions for future years.

  • Data gathered will be made 100% open and free to use for all artists
  • Check out our open repository on GitHub

For the time being this landing page constitutes the extent of the open-source wing of the project while the survey itself is temporarily managed through Google Forms which is free but unfortunately not open source. After the survey is complete and in future iterations a fully open source survey platform will be implemented.
